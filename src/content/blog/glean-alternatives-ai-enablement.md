            *Glean is impressive — $200M+ ARR, 100+ integrations, the most sophisticated enterprise context graph in the market. But it's built for the Fortune 500. Here are 7 alternatives for companies that need AI enablement without the enterprise price tag or 6-month integration timeline.*

            Glean is the gold standard for enterprise AI search. Their Spring '26 launch — agent sandboxes, 85+ autonomous actions, personal graphs, voice support — is the most ambitious product expansion in the category.

            But Glean's strengths are also its limitations. It requires deep integration across your entire tech stack. Implementation takes months. Pricing is enterprise-tier. And for the 99% of companies that aren't Fortune 500, that's a non-starter.

            If you're looking for Glean alternatives, the right question isn't "what's cheaper?" — it's **"what approach to AI actually fits my company's size, budget, and readiness?"**

## Detailed Comparison

            
### 1. iEnable — Best for Full AI Enablement

            **What it does:** Gives every employee a personal AI enabler — a named, role-specific AI that learns your business, coordinates across departments, and works within governance guardrails. Not just search — execution.

            **How it differs from Glean:** Glean creates a searchable layer on top of your tools. iEnable creates an AI workforce that actively works alongside your team — researching, analyzing, producing, and recommending actions with human approval.

            <table><thead><tr><th>Feature</th><th>Glean</th><th>iEnable</th></tr></thead><tbody>
                <tr><td>**Deployment**</td><td>3-6 month integration</td><td>90-second scan, same-day</td></tr>
                <tr><td>**AI approach**</td><td>Search your data, ask questions</td><td>Per-employee enablers that learn and execute</td></tr>
                <tr><td>**Governance**</td><td>Enterprise admin controls</td><td>Tiered approval chains, spending limits, audit trails</td></tr>
                <tr><td>**Learning**</td><td>Graph improves search relevance</td><td>Tracks outcomes, builds playbooks, promotes validated findings</td></tr>
                <tr><td>**Coordination**</td><td>100+ app connectors</td><td>Agent-to-agent coordination, automated routing</td></tr>
                <tr><td>**Pricing**</td><td>$200K+/year enterprise</td><td>Free start, value-based scaling</td></tr>
            </tbody></table>
            

#### Pros

- Fastest time to value (90 seconds)
- Per-employee personalization
- Built-in governance and approval
- Compound learning — AI gets smarter
- Accessible pricing for any company

#### Cons

- Newer platform (less third-party validation)
- Smaller integration ecosystem

            **Best for:** Companies that want AI enablement, not just AI search. Especially SMBs, mid-market, companies needing governance from day one.

            **Why users switch from Glean:** Implementation complexity, enterprise-only pricing, wanting execution capabilities beyond search.

            
### 2. Microsoft Copilot — Best for Microsoft-Native Orgs

            **What it does:** AI assistant embedded in Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Outlook, Teams. Recently launched [Copilot Tasks](copilot-tasks-vs-ai-enablement.html) for autonomous agent capabilities.

            <table><thead><tr><th>Feature</th><th>Glean</th><th>Microsoft Copilot</th></tr></thead><tbody>
                <tr><td>**Integration**</td><td>100+ apps</td><td>Microsoft 365 ecosystem</td></tr>
                <tr><td>**Agents**</td><td>Sandboxes + 85+ actions</td><td>Copilot Tasks (research preview)</td></tr>
                <tr><td>**Pricing**</td><td>Enterprise contract</td><td>$30/user/month</td></tr>
                <tr><td>**Deployment**</td><td>Months</td><td>Weeks</td></tr>
            </tbody></table>
            

#### Pros

- Native Office 365 integration
- Familiar interface
- $30/user/month pricing
- Copilot Tasks adds agents

#### Cons

- Limited to Microsoft ecosystem
- Binary governance only
- No compound learning
- 72% struggle to adopt (Gartner)

            **Best for:** Organizations fully committed to Microsoft 365.

            
### 3. GoSearch — Best Affordable Enterprise Search

            **What it does:** AI-powered enterprise search with federated and indexed approaches. 100+ connectors. Transparent pricing.

            <table><thead><tr><th>Feature</th><th>Glean</th><th>GoSearch</th></tr></thead><tbody>
                <tr><td>**Pricing**</td><td>$200K+/year</td><td>$20/user/month</td></tr>
                <tr><td>**Security**</td><td>Enterprise-grade</td><td>SOC 2 Type II</td></tr>
                <tr><td>**Deployment**</td><td>Months</td><td>1-2 weeks</td></tr>
            </tbody></table>
            

#### Pros

- Most affordable Glean-like search
- Transparent pricing
- Fast deployment
- SOC 2 Type II

#### Cons

- Search-focused, limited agents
- No compound learning
- No per-employee personalization

            **Best for:** Companies needing enterprise search at a fraction of Glean's price.

            
### 4. Coveo — Best for E-Commerce &amp; CX

            **What it does:** AI search and recommendations with behavioral AI. Deep Salesforce integration.

            

#### Pros

- Best-in-class e-commerce search
- Behavioral AI at scale
- Deep Salesforce integration

#### Cons

- Customer-facing focus
- Premium pricing
- Steep learning curve

            **Best for:** Large enterprises focused on customer experience and e-commerce.

            
### 5. HubSpot Breeze AI — Best for SMB Marketing

            **What it does:** AI agents native to HubSpot CRM — prospecting, content, and customer service agents.

            

#### Pros

- Included with HubSpot
- Native CRM integration
- Fast to deploy

#### Cons

- HubSpot-only
- Marketing/sales focus only
- No governance layer

            **Best for:** SMBs on HubSpot wanting AI marketing and sales automation.

            
### 6. Relevance AI — Best for Custom Agent Builders

            **What it does:** Platform for building custom AI agents with multi-step workflows and tool integrations.

            

#### Pros

- Most customizable
- Starting at $19/month
- Calendar-triggered workflows

#### Cons

- Requires technical skill
- No enterprise search
- Limited cross-agent coordination

            **Best for:** Technical teams and startups building custom AI agents.

            
### 7. Elastic — Best for Engineering Teams

            **What it does:** Configurable search infrastructure on Apache Lucene. Hybrid keyword + semantic search at massive scale.

            

#### Pros

- Most scalable and customizable
- Self-hosted option
- Real-time analytics

#### Cons

- Requires engineering team
- Not a packaged product
- No enablement or agents

            **Best for:** Engineering-heavy organizations with custom search needs.
