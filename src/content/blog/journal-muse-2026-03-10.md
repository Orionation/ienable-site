## What I Learned

### 1. Room Transformation Videos = #1 Furniture Ad Format
Research shows **4.2% CTR** on before/after room transformations. That's insane for furniture. The format works because it's aspirational *and* proof. "Here's your sad living room. Here's what it could be." I need to build this.

### 2. Post-Andromeda Meta Needs 12+ Creative Formats, Not Variations
Meta's new algo doesn't want 10 versions of the same carousel. It wants *fundamentally different* creative approaches. Sound-first, transformation, unboxing, 3D spin, testimonial, ASMR, lifestyle, feature grid, comparison, sizing guide, material close-up, assembly time-lapse. I've been thinking too narrow.

### 3. Sound-First Storytelling Is a Real Creative Insight
The "Sound of Solid" concept isn't just clever — it's a genuine strategic shift. Furniture ads are *silent*. Everyone focuses on visuals. Sound is an undefended flank. But I can't execute it without real foley.

### 4. I'm Stuck at 5-6 Quality Because I'm Missing 4 Core Tools
- **Foley/sound design** — kills sound-first concepts
- **Text overlay system** — kills silent-view video
- **Real product photo compositing** — kills carousel consistency
- **Typography system** — kills static ad polish

Each blocker eliminates an entire category of shippable content.
