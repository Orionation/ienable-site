👥 Audience Overlap

February 28, 2026

⚠️ Significant Waste Detected

Multiple campaigns are targeting the same audiences, creating auction competition against ourselves and inflating CPMs. We're bidding against our own campaigns.

🎨 MoF Consideration ↔️ 🎯 Retargeting 47%
Problem: MoF Consideration (1.03x ROAS) is hitting the same people as Retargeting (20.61x ROAS). We're wasting money showing ads to people who would convert via the cheaper, higher-ROAS retargeting campaign.
Impact: ~$34K/month wasted on duplicate reach. MoF is winning auctions over Retargeting in 47% of cases, delivering 20x worse ROAS.
❌ MoF DPA-Only ↔️ 🎨 MoF Consideration 68%
Problem: DPA-Only (0.73x ROAS, losing money) has 68% overlap with MoF Consideration (1.03x ROAS). Two low-performing campaigns competing for the same eyeballs.
Impact: Bidding against ourselves in 68% of impressions. Inflating CPMs for both campaigns. DPA-Only should be killed regardless, but overlap makes it worse.
🚀 ASC+ Promo ↔️ 🎯 Retargeting 23%
Status: Minor overlap. Both campaigns are high-ROAS (17.08x and 20.61x), so this isn't critical. However, could reduce waste by excluding ASC+ from retargeting audiences.
Impact: ~$6K/month potential savings. Low priority compared to MoF issues.
🚨 Root Cause: MoF campaigns (Consideration and DPA-Only) lack audience exclusions. They're broad campaigns hitting everyone, including people already in retargeting pools. This creates self-competition.

💡 Consolidation Strategy

1. Kill MoF DPA-Only immediately
0.73x ROAS, 68% overlap with MoF Consideration. No reason to keep it alive. Saves $10.2K/month.
2. Exclude retargeting audiences from MoF Consideration
Add audience exclusion: "Website visitors (last 30 days)". This prevents MoF from competing with Retargeting's 20.61x ROAS. Saves ~$34K/month.
3. Consolidate MoF spend into ASC+ and Retargeting
Shift saved budget ($44K total) to campaigns with proven 17-20x ROAS. Projected impact: +$750K/month revenue.
4. Weekly overlap monitoring
Check audience overlap reports every Monday. Flag any campaign with >30% overlap. Prevents future waste.
📊 Expected Outcome: Removing overlap saves $44K/month in wasted spend. Reallocating that to 17-20x ROAS campaigns yields $750K-880K/month incremental revenue. This is a single structural fix with massive compounding impact.
